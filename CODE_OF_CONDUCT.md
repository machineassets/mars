# Code of Conduct

**Machine Assets Reporting Standard — Community Standards**

---

## Our Standard

MARS is a technical standards project. Contributions are evaluated on evidence and reasoning, not on who submits them. We expect all participants — regardless of background, experience level, or affiliation — to engage with honesty and respect.

---

## Expected Behaviour

**Be precise.** When you propose a change, state exactly what should change and why. Vague objections are not useful. Specific, evidenced critique is.

**Be honest about uncertainty.** If you are not sure, say so. MARS explicitly labels estimates as estimates. Contributors should do the same.

**Engage with objections.** If someone challenges your MEP, respond to the substance of their argument. Ignoring technical objections is not acceptable.

**Declare conflicts of interest.** If you have a commercial interest in a proposed change, declare it at the start of the discussion. This does not disqualify you — it gives the community context.

**Credit prior work.** If your proposal builds on someone else's analysis or data, acknowledge it.

---

## Unacceptable Behaviour

- Personal attacks, insults, or dismissiveness toward contributors
- Submitting proposals designed to advantage a specific manufacturer, insurer, or operator without disclosure
- Misrepresenting data, citations, or the current state of the standard
- Harassment of any kind, in GitHub Discussions or any other channel
- Attempts to make the standard proprietary or manufacturer-dependent

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all MARS community spaces including:

- GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, and Discussions
- Email correspondence with standards@machineassets.ai
- Any forum or event where MARS is being formally discussed

---

## Enforcement

Violations should be reported to [standards@machineassets.ai](mailto:standards@machineassets.ai).

All reports will be reviewed. Responses may include a warning, temporary suspension from discussions, or permanent removal from the project depending on severity.

The maintainer's decisions on enforcement are final.
